Agentic AI amplifies whatever your engineering culture already is. Disciplined or chaotic.

This is the single most important thing to understand about Agentic AI adoption. It is not a transformation tool. It is an accelerant. Organisations with strong delivery practices, clear service boundaries, good test coverage, well-defined contracts, and teams that own their deployments end-to-end will find that agents accelerate good work. Organisations without those foundations will find that agents accelerate entropy: more code, produced faster, with less understanding of what it does or why.

The implication is uncomfortable but necessary: you cannot adopt Agentic AI effectively without strong delivery practices. And modern delivery practices are increasingly AI-native. These are not two problems. They are one.

This means the work of "setting up Agentic AI dev teams" is not primarily a tooling exercise. It is a delivery capability exercise that happens to involve new tools. The tools are the least interesting part.
